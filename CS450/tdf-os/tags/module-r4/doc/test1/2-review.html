7. Explain briefly why the objectives of a batch operating system conflict with those 

of an interactive system. 


A batch system relies solely on system input and accept no human interaction.  Batch systems

take advantage of computing resources more efficiently as they do not have to idle for human

input.  An interactive system has to have a layer for interacting with a user, even during execution, which requires an entirely different design model.


8. What is unusual about the manner in which the UNIX operating system was 

developed? 


UNIX was developed solely by researchers for their own use.  It wasn't meant

to be distributed.  The licensing issue was interesting because IBM had no claim

to the operation system as they weren't in the business of operating systems and

then just gave it away as a result.  


9. Give a principal reason why the VAX was developed. 


VAX was developed as a result to the limitations of the PDP-11.


10. Why were the goals of operating systems for local area networks different than 

those for long-distance networks?  


Long distance networks require more management of computing resources that weren't local.  Long distance networks needed to serve different computers with different objectives.
